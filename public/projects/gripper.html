Apple Picking Gripper

Mechanical Design SolidWorks FEA 3D Printing

Overview

This project is my final year engineering project and focuses on the design of a robotic end-effector for automated apple harvesting. The work is being conducted in collaboration with the MARS team (Monash Apple Retrieval System).

The objective of the project is to redesign the existing robotic gripper to reduce its physical footprint. A smaller and more compact end effector allows the harvesting robot to reach further into orchard canopies while reducing the likelihood of collisions with branches and surrounding apples.

The design must also ensure reliable fruit grasping while minimising damage to both the harvested apple and neighbouring fruit. The project involves mechanical design, structural analysis, and iterative prototyping to evaluate different gripper geometries and mechanisms.

The project is currently in progress, with ongoing design refinement and prototype testing.

Key Aspects of the Project

Structural Analysis

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) is used to evaluate stresses and structural behaviour of the gripper components during operation.

Prototype Development

Rapid prototyping using 3D printing allows for iterative testing and refinement of different mechanical designs.

Agricultural Robotics

The project contributes to the development of robotic harvesting systems designed to improve efficiency and reduce labour requirements in orchard environments.
